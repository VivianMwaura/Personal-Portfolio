Chapati and Beans Stew Recipe

A delicious and wholesome meal perfect for any time of the day!

Ingredients

For the Chapati:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil or ghee
  • Warm water (as needed)

For the Beans Stew:

  • 1 cup dried beans (kidney beans or any preferred type)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Water (as needed)
  • Fresh coriander (for garnish, optional)

Instructions

Making the Chapati:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the oil and mix well.
  2. Gradually add warm water, kneading until you form a soft and pliable dough.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin circle on a floured surface.
  5. Heat a skillet or pan over medium heat. Cook each chapati for about 1-2 minutes on each side, until golden brown and slightly puffed.

Making the Beans Stew:

  1. Soak the dried beans overnight or for at least 4 hours. Drain and rinse.
  2. In a pot, add the soaked beans and enough water to cover them. Cook until tender (about 1-1.5 hours). Drain and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions and sauté until soft.
  4. Add garlic, ginger, and chopped tomatoes, and cook until the tomatoes are soft.
  5. Add the cooked beans to the pot. Stir in cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Add water to reach desired consistency.
  6. Simmer for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Serve the warm chapati alongside the beans stew. Enjoy your delicious and hearty meal!
